Interested in vending, speaking, or healing at the next DIY-HF? Wonderful! We would love to have you. First, take a look at the rules and guidelines below, and please keep in mind that by applying to be a participant, you are agreeing to the following terms as we have outlined them.

Application Overview

  1. By applying for the DIY-HF, you agree to act in a respectful manner towards your fellow participants and visitors to the Flea. You agree to follow all guidelines set forth by the host venue. Any behavior deemed disrespectful or that goes against the policies of the host venue will result in a permanent ban from participating in future fleas. Just be cool and there should be no issues! Our goal is to heal with one another, after all.

  2. We are strictly an anti-racist, pro-queer, radically oriented event and we hold space for that. Any behavior that goes against this will result in a ban.

  3. All participants must be engaged in a practice of healing, mending, or repair in some respect. We are open to hearing however you define this!

We process applications in batches several weeks to a month prior to the next event. Application windows are shown in the table below. You can only apply for the upcoming event; to apply for a farther future event, please wait until the current application deadline has passed.

Vendor Registration

The role of Vendor is for people who want to sell their own handmade wares or basic services at the Flea. As a vendor, you will be expected to:

  1. pay a non-refundable tabling fee, which is listed on our home page before each Flea.

  2. arrive one hour before the posted start time of the Flea.

  3. bring your own table, no larger than 48 inches long by 24 inches wide unless otherwise specified, along with any and all other materials that you will need.

Although not required, we strongly encourage you to make use of our Press Kit to publish promotions and help us get the word out about the next Flea! This helps ensure you and all the other vendors have a successful Flea market.

Ready to apply to vend at the next DIY Healer’s Flea? Continue to the Vendor Application.

Speaker Registration

The role of Speaker is for people who want to give a talk or lead a brief workshop on a topic that they know a bit about. As a speaker, you will be expected to:

  1. offer your talk or workshop freely to all attendees of the Flea. (You may request donations for your talk, but only at the end.)

  2. clean up after your talk or workshop so that your allocated space is in the same or better condition than it was when it was given to you.

  3. fit your entire session within your allotted time. This includes set-up, tear-down, and cleaning. Running late eats into other speakers’ time and is highly disruptive.

  4. bring all materials you need for your talk or workshop with you; there are no guarantees that any specific equipment will be available at the host venue.

  5. use your time slot to focus on skill-sharing and mutual self-education, not to advertise your wares; that’s what a vendor registration is for. You’re welcome to plug your work, but please keep it brief and do it towards the end of your session time.

Although not required, we strongly encourage you to make use of our Press Kit to publish promotions and help us get the word out about the next Flea! This helps ensure you and all the other speakers have a successful Flea market.
